About Me

Entrepreneur, Builder, BYU Student

I'm Caleb Cooper, a student at Brigham Young University studying Entrepreneur Management. I'm passionate about building real things that solve real problems and learning through doing.

I co-founded Oni Nets, where I’ve been able to take an idea from concept to product. Through that process, I’ve learned about product design, branding, and what it takes to bring something into the real world.

Before BYU, I worked as a paralegal at Cooper|Coons, which helped me develop attention to detail and strong communication skills. That experience continues to influence how I approach business and decision-making.

I also served as a missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and am now fluent in Portuguese. That experience shaped how I work with people and gave me a global perspective that I bring into everything I do.

Outside of school and business, I enjoy surfing, staying active, golfing, and spending time with friends and family. I'm always looking for new adventures and constantly learning new skills to help me become a better person.
